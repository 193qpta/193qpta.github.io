# PS 193Q PTA Bylaws: Comparative Analysis with Peer NYC Schools

**Prepared for the Bylaws Revision Committee**
**March 2026**

Schools compared: PS 9 (Brooklyn), PS 11 Chelsea (Manhattan), PS 20 Clinton Hill (Brooklyn), PS 24 Sunset Park (Brooklyn), PS 31 (Brooklyn), PS 34 (Brooklyn), PS 53K (Brooklyn), PS 186Q (Queens)

> Note: D26 Presidents' Council bylaws are also on file for reference, but as a district-level body they are not directly comparable to school-level PA/PTA bylaws.

| School | Limit | Notes |

| PS 9 (Brooklyn) | 2 terms | |
| PS 53K (Brooklyn) | 2 terms | |
| PS 24 | 2 terms | |
| PS 31 | 2 terms | 3rd only if no other candidate |
| PS 34 | 3 terms | |

| PS 20 (Clinton Hill) | 4 terms | Co-officer exception: one co-officer can exceed limits if the other hasn't |

## 2. Non-Mandatory Officer Positions

This is where schools differ most from the template — and where PS 193Q has the biggest gap between bylaws and practice.

| School | Non-Mandatory Positions |
|--------|------------------------|
| **PS 193Q (bylaws)** | VP, Corresponding Secretary, Parliamentarian, Sergeant at Arms |
| **PS 193Q (actual practice)** | Corresponding Secretary, Parent Volunteer Coordinator, Hospitality, Membership/Tech, Fundraising |
| PS 9 | VP Events, VP Fundraising, VP Admin-PTO Coordination, 2nd Recording Secretary, Corresponding Secretary, Financial Secretary, Parliamentarian, Lower- & Upper-Grade Parent-Teacher Liaisons |
| PS 11 Chelsea | VP Community Membership, VP Community Programs, VP Community Events, VP Fund-Raising, VP Communications, Assistant Treasurer, Members-at-Large (2) |
| PS 20 | VP, Corresponding Secretary, Financial Secretary, Parliamentarian, Parent Outreach Correspondent, Member at Large |
| PS 24 | VP, Communications Secretary, Parliamentarian, Director of Events, Community Liaison |
| PS 34 | VP, Corresponding Secretary, Parliamentarian, Sergeant at Arms *(template default)* |
| PS 53K | VP, Corresponding Secretary, Parliamentarian, Sergeant at Arms *(template default, non-mandatory section largely unfilled)* |
| PS 186Q | VP, Corresponding Secretary, Financial Secretary *(partially customized)* |

**Takeaway:** Schools that function well formalize positions around actual operational needs. Schools that kept the template defaults (PS 34, PS 53K) tend to have the least specificity. PS 193Q's informal positions (Hospitality, Fundraising, Membership/Tech, Volunteer Coordinator) should be written into the bylaws.

## 3. Co-Officer Rules

| School | Approach |
|--------|----------|
| **PS 193Q** | Mentions co-officers in some titles but has no formal policy |
| PS 20 | Explicit: any position may be shared; co-officers must agree in advance and run as a slate |
| PS 11 Chelsea | Every position has "or Co-" language with detailed rules |
| PS 9 | No co-officer provisions |
| PS 186Q | No formal co-officer policy |
| PS 53K | No formal co-officer policy |

**Takeaway:** PS 20 has the cleanest model — flexible but with clear slate rules.

## 4. Executive Board Quorum

| School | EB Quorum |
|--------|-----------|
| PS 31 | 2 board members |
| PS 24 | 2 mandatory officers |
| PS 20 | 3 members |
| PS 53K | 3 members |
| PS 186Q | 3 members |
| PS 9 | Simple majority + 1 mandatory officer |
| PS 11 Chelsea | More than half of EB |
| **PS 193Q** | **"Cannot be fewer than 8 persons"** |

**Takeaway:** PS 193Q's requirement of 8 is the highest of any school reviewed and may be a significant source of dysfunction. 3 members is the most common choice.

## 7. Voluntary Donation Amount

| School | Amount | Language |
|--------|--------|---------|
| **PS 193Q** | **$25.00 per family** | Hard-coded amount |
| PS 9 | No amount specified | "The PTO may make donation requests of members; such donations are entirely voluntary." |
| PS 11 Chelsea | No amount specified | "Each member may be requested to make a voluntary donation." |
| PS 20 | No amount specified | "From time to time, members may be requested to make a voluntary donation." |
| PS 24 | $10 per family | Hard-coded amount, described as voluntary |
| PS 31 | ~$200 per student (target) | "The PTA targets approximately $200 per student." Explicitly stated as never required. |
| PS 34 | $25 | Hard-coded amount |
| PS 53K | N/A | "$n/a" — no donation requested |
| PS 186Q | $25.00 | Hard-coded amount |

**Takeaway:** Schools split roughly evenly between hard-coding an amount and leaving it flexible. The more carefully drafted bylaws (PS 9, PS 11 Chelsea, PS 20) avoid hard-coding a number, which means the board can adjust the ask year-to-year without a bylaws amendment. All schools emphasize that payment is never a condition for membership or voting.

## 9. Unique Provisions Not in PS 193Q

| Feature | School(s) | What it does |
|---------|-----------|--------------|
| **Indemnification clause** | PS 20 | Protects officers against legal liability for good-faith actions |
| **A-660 supremacy clause** | PS 20 | Explicitly states regulation overrides any conflicting bylaw |
| **Governance Committee** | PS 9 | Standing committee to maintain bylaws and onboard new officers |
| **Conflicts of Interest Policy** | PS 31 | Separate detailed policy with annual certification |
| **Candidate statements** | PS 9 | Nominating committee solicits and distributes statements to membership |
| **Campaigning rules** | PS 11 Chelsea | Prohibits distributing materials on school premises |
| **Tax reporting duty** | PS 9 | Treasurer explicitly responsible for annual tax filings |
| **Non-mandatory officers elected in fall** | PS 9 | Mandatory spring, non-mandatory can wait until fall |
| **Flexible Fund** | PS 9 | Board-discretionary budget line with equity consideration |
| **Committee budget reporting** | PS 20 | Committees with budgets must submit proposals by October |
| **Members-at-Large** | PS 11 Chelsea | 2 positions for incoming parents, elected in October |
| **Virtual election procedures** | PS 186Q | Detailed anonymous poll procedures for virtual contested elections |
| **Single debit card policy** | PS 186Q | Only ONE debit card allowed; all others must be destroyed with a signed record |
| **Online banking disbursement protocols** | PS 186Q | Detailed Online Payment Disbursement Form requirements with dual signatory |

1. **Term limit number blank** (Art. IV.B.2)
2. **Succession order blank** (Art. IV.D.7.c) — still has template brackets
