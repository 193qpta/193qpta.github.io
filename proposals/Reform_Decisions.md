## Proposal 1: Add A-660 Supremacy Clause

**Status:** Proposed
**Decision Date:** *Pending committee review*
**Model:** PS 20 Clinton Hill (2024 bylaws, Article II, item 6)
**Current bylaws reference:** Article II (Objectives) — no equivalent provision exists

### Rationale
The board frequently refers back to Chancellor's Regulation A-660 to resolve questions. An explicit supremacy clause avoids ambiguity about what happens if a bylaw provision inadvertently conflicts with the regulation, and protects the Association from adopting a provision that turns out to be non-compliant.

### Proposed Language
Add as item 6 under Article II (Objectives):

> 6\. Comply with Chancellor's Regulation A-660. Although the Association has attempted to ensure that these bylaws comply with Chancellor's Regulation A-660, the regulation shall control over provisions in these bylaws that violate Chancellor's Regulation A-660 (if any). For the avoidance of doubt, if any provisions in these bylaws are impermissible under Chancellor's Regulation A-660, every permissible provision in these bylaws shall remain in place.

### Impact
- No operational change — formalizes existing practice
- Provides legal clarity if a dispute arises

## Proposal 2: Formalize Co-Officer Policy

**Status:** Decided — Approved
**Decision Date:** March 30, 2026
**Model:** PS 20 Clinton Hill (2024 bylaws, Article IV.A.3)
**Current bylaws reference:** Article IV.A (Titles) — co-officers mentioned in some duty titles but no formal rules exist; Article IV.B (Term Limits) — no co-officer provision; Article IV.D.7 (Vacancies) — no co-officer provision

### Rationale
PS 193Q already operates with co-officers (co-presidents, co-treasurers) but the bylaws have no formal rules governing how co-officers run for office, share duties, or handle vacancies. This has led to confusion about responsibilities. Formalizing the rules creates clarity and prevents disputes.

### Proposed Language

**Add as item 3 under Article IV, Section A (Titles):**

> 3\. Any or all of the Association's officer positions may be shared with a co-officer. A co-officer will not be required for any position.
>
> a. An individual who runs for a co-officer position must agree in advance to run with a designated co-officer candidate. The two co-officer candidates will then run as a slate, and neither can be elected for an officer position individually — they can only be elected as co-officers.
>
> b. For example, the board officers might consist of Co-Presidents, Co-Recording Secretaries, Co-Treasurers, and an individual officer at each of the other positions, if two people run for President, Recording Secretary, and Treasurer and each agree and confirm that they are willing to share the role with a designated co-officer in advance, and one person runs for each of the other board positions.

**Add to Article IV, Section B (Term and Term Limits):**

> 3\. Notwithstanding any other provision to the contrary, term limits may be exceeded through a co-officer position as long as one of the co-officers for the same position has not exceeded their term limits. If both co-officer candidates have exceeded their term limit, then they may be elected to serve an additional term only if no other interested candidate is nominated and willing to serve.

**Add to Article IV, Section D.7 (Officer Vacancies):**

> d. Upon the resignation or removal of a co-officer, the remaining co-officer must choose whether to serve alone for the remainder of the term or resign so that the position can be filled by succession or expedited election.

### Impact
- Formalizes how PS 193Q already operates
- Provides clear rules for elections, vacancies, and term limits involving co-officers
- Eliminates ambiguity about shared responsibilities

## Proposal 3: Restructure Non-Mandatory Officer Positions

**Status:** Proposed — Under review, to be discussed at next committee meeting
**Decision Date:** *Pending*

### Committee Feedback (March 30, 2026)
- Initial feedback positive; fundraising split was well received
- **Amy** raised concern about further board consolidation reducing "hands to help" — merging positions could mean fewer people doing the work
- **Melissa** raised concern that if the PTA reduces its fundraisers in the future, the named fundraising positions may not map well to actual needs
- Nothing was rejected outright; proposal disseminated for review ahead of next meeting
**Current bylaws reference:** Article IV.A.2 (Titles — non-mandatory officers); Article IV.C.4 (Duties — non-mandatory officers); Article V.A (Executive Board composition — blank placeholder)

### Rationale
The bylaws list VP, Corresponding Secretary, Parliamentarian, and Sergeant at Arms as non-mandatory positions. None of these are currently filled. Meanwhile, the board actually operates with Corresponding Secretary, Parent Volunteer Coordinator, Hospitality, Membership/Tech, and Fundraising — none of which (except Corresponding Secretary) are in the bylaws. This mismatch is a root cause of unclear and uneven responsibilities. The proposal restructures positions around actual operations and splits vague roles into clear domains.

### Proposed Changes
See `Position_Matrix.md` for the full before/after breakdown. Summary:

- Merge Corresponding Secretary + tech duties → **Corresponding Secretary/Tech**
- Merge Membership + Volunteer Coordinator → **Membership/Volunteer**
- Keep **Hospitality** as-is
- Split Fundraising (currently up to 4 people, unclear duties) into 3 named positions:
  - **Fundraising - Shops** (Book fair, Holiday shops, apparel)
  - **Fundraising - Lunch Period** (Fun Friday, Pizza Day)
  - **Fundraising - Dances & Events** (Halloween Dance, Spring Fling, large fundraisers)
- Add new: **Educational & Social Programming** (Charity walk, Residency, Fall Festival, Carnival, Welcome Back Party, grant applications)
- Remove unused: VP, Parliamentarian, Sergeant at Arms

### Impact
- 7 non-mandatory positions replacing current mix of 5 informal + 3 unused
- Every position has a clear name, domain, and no overlapping responsibilities
- Directly addresses the primary source of board acrimony

## Proposal 4: Set Term Limits at 2 Consecutive Terms

**Status:** Decided — Approved
**Decision Date:** March 30, 2026
**Current bylaws reference:** Article IV.B.2 (Term and Term Limits) — number currently blank

### Rationale
The current bylaws say "consecutive one year terms" but never specify how many. This effectively means there are no term limits. Setting a clear number encourages leadership turnover while still allowing continuity when no new candidates step up.

### Proposed Language
Replace Article IV, Section B.2 with:

> 2\. Term limits for each officer position of the Association shall be 2 consecutive one-year terms. A candidate who has served the maximum number of consecutive terms in a position may only be elected to serve an additional term in that same position if no other interested candidate is nominated and willing to serve.

### Impact
- Fills in a blank that has existed since the bylaws were first adopted
- Aligns with the most common choice among peer schools (PS 9, PS 24, PS 31, PS 53K all use 2 terms)
- Preserves the safety valve for uncontested positions

## Proposal 5: Reduce Executive Board Quorum to 5 Members

**Status:** Decided — Approved
**Decision Date:** March 30, 2026
**Current bylaws reference:** Article V.D (Quorum) — currently "cannot be fewer than 8 persons"

### Rationale
The current EB quorum of 8 is the highest of any peer school reviewed and makes it difficult to conduct board business, particularly with a small board. Reducing to 5 is still above the peer average of 3 but provides a more workable threshold.

### Proposed Language
Replace Article V, Section D with:

> D. Quorum
>
> Five members of the executive board shall constitute a quorum, allowing for official business to be transacted.

### Impact
- Reduces the barrier to conducting EB business from 8 to 5
- Still above the most common peer choice of 3, maintaining a reasonable participation standard

## Proposal 7: Remove Hard-Coded Donation Amount

**Status:** Decided — Approved
**Decision Date:** March 30, 2026
**Current bylaws reference:** Article III.B.2 (Dues/Donations) — currently "$25.00 per family"

### Rationale
Hard-coding a dollar amount in the bylaws requires a bylaws amendment to change the ask. Removing the specific number lets the board set the donation request each year based on budgetary needs without a formal amendment process.

### Proposed Language
Replace Article III, Section B.2 with:

> 2\. Members may be requested to make a voluntary donation. The amount of the suggested donation shall be determined by the executive board based on the Association's budgetary needs.

### Impact
- Board can adjust the donation request year-to-year without a bylaws amendment
- Aligns with approach used by PS 9, PS 11 Chelsea, and PS 20
- No change to the rule that dues/donations are never a condition for membership or voting

## Proposal 8: Add Indemnification Clause

**Status:** Proposed — Committee expressed interest; to be discussed at next meeting
**Decision Date:** *Pending*
**Model:** PS 20 Clinton Hill (2024 bylaws, Article IX)
**Current bylaws reference:** N/A — no equivalent provision exists

### Rationale
Officers volunteer their time and can face personal legal exposure for good-faith decisions made on behalf of the Association. An indemnification clause protects past and present officers against expenses and liabilities from legal proceedings related to their service, provided they did not engage in willful misconduct or gross negligence.

### Proposed Language (from PS 20)

> The Association may, to the extent legally permissible, indemnify any past or present officer, director, eligible member, or volunteer against all expenses and liabilities reasonably incurred in connection with actual or threatened legal proceedings by reason of his or her service in such capacity, provided that that individual has not engaged in willful misconduct or gross negligence that directly or indirectly led to the named or threatened action. Reasonable expenses shall include judgments, fines, penalties, settlements, and reasonable attorneys' fees incurred by an attorney selected by the PTA. This provision shall not limit the PTA's ability to otherwise indemnify individuals at its discretion.

### Impact
- Protects volunteers from personal financial risk for good-faith service
- Standard nonprofit governance provision
- No cost to the Association unless a claim actually arises

## Proposal 9: Create Incoming Parent Position Opportunities

**Status:** Proposed — Committee expressed interest; to be discussed at next meeting
**Decision Date:** *Pending*
**Model:** PS 11 Chelsea (2023 bylaws, Article IV, Section 3.10 — Members-at-Large)
**Current bylaws reference:** N/A — no equivalent provision exists

### Rationale
New families often want to get involved but are unfamiliar with the PTA and hesitant to run for a named officer position. Creating a designated pathway for incoming parents lowers the barrier to board participation and builds the pipeline of future officers.

### Concept
Create one or more Member-at-Large positions reserved for parents new to the school. These positions would:
- Be elected in the fall (October) rather than spring, giving new families time to settle in
- Have lighter duties (assist with events, fundraising, etc.) as an on-ramp to future leadership
- Serve on the executive board with voting rights

### Open Questions for Committee
- How many positions? (PS 11 Chelsea uses 2)
- Should they be restricted to parents in their first year at the school?
- What specific duties, if any, should be assigned?
