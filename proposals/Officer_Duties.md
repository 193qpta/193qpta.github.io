# PS 193Q PTA: Officer Duties — Proposed Bylaws Language

> This document contains the proposed language for **Article IV, Section C.4 — Non-Mandatory Officers** as part of Proposal 3 in `Reform_Decisions.md`. The text below is intended to replace Article IV, Section C.4 of the existing bylaws in its entirety.
>
> **Status:** Adopted — incorporated into Bylaws_2026_Draft.md
> **Last updated:** April 19, 2026

---

## Scope

This document covers the **non-mandatory** officer positions only. The duties of mandatory officers (President, Recording Secretary, Treasurer) are already detailed in the existing bylaws (Article IV, Section C.1–3) and are unchanged by Proposal 3.

Duty language is adapted from the supplemental document `193_bylaws/Executive_Board_Positions_Clean.md` and updated to reflect committee decisions made on March 30, April 7, and April 10, 2026. PS 193Q-specific quirks have been generalized where appropriate so the bylaws remain durable across changes in tools and operations.

---

## Article IV, Section C.4 — Non-Mandatory Officers

### Corresponding Secretary

The duties of the Corresponding Secretary shall include but are not limited to the following:

a. Make all flyers and copies for the Association and prepare them for distribution;

b. Secure approval from the school administration for all flyers prior to distribution;

c. Assist the Recording Secretary, and shall assume the Recording Secretary's duties in their absence or upon request;

d. May be one of the eligible signatories on checks and disbursement forms;

e. Assist with the June transfer of Association records, including all pertinent user IDs and passwords, to the incoming executive board.

### Social Media and Membership

The duties of the Social Media and Membership officer shall include but are not limited to the following:

a. Maintain the Association's social media accounts and post regular updates regarding meetings, fundraisers, and school events;

b. Review flyers prepared by the Corresponding Secretary prior to distribution;

c. Maintain the Association's online communication and meeting platforms, including any video conferencing accounts used for virtual meetings, and assist in hosting virtual general membership meetings;

d. Prepare and update membership forms and invitations to join prior to the beginning of each school year;

e. Coordinate the collection of voluntary donations in early September and forward all collected funds to the Treasurer;

f. Create and maintain the Association's membership roster.

### Parent Volunteer Coordinator

The duties of the Parent Volunteer Coordinator shall include but are not limited to the following:

a. Collect and organize parent volunteer information for the Association;

b. Organize volunteer schedules for fundraisers and Association events;

c. Work with the school administration to obtain the list of Class Parents (parents who volunteer to help with class activities), organize them, and host an annual meeting in October to introduce Class Parents to the Association;

d. Coordinate parent volunteers for school health screenings and similar activities requested by the school administration.

### Fundraising

The Fundraising position may be filled by up to four (4) individuals, each elected individually. The Fundraising position is not subject to the co-officer policy set forth in Article IV, Section A.3; Fundraising officers do not run as co-officer slates.

The duties of the Fundraising officer(s) shall include but are not limited to the following:

a. Plan and organize all Association fundraising activities, including but not limited to book fairs, holiday shops, food sales, dances, and similar events. All Fundraising officers shall plan and execute fundraising activities jointly;

b. Develop and propose new fundraising ideas to the executive board for discussion and approval;

c. Develop and maintain community relationships in support of Association fundraising;

d. Collect funds raised at fundraising events and reconcile totals with the Treasurer;

e. Prepare raffle prizes and other materials needed for fundraising events.

### Educational & Social Programming

The duties of the Educational & Social Programming officer shall include but are not limited to the following:

a. Plan and organize the Association's educational and social events, including but not limited to the Charity walk, school Residency programs, Fall Festival, Carnival, Welcome Back Party, and similar events;

b. Identify and apply for grants on behalf of the Association;

c. Coordinate with the school administration regarding educational and social programming;

d. Bring proposals for new educational or social programming to the executive board for discussion and approval.

### 5th Grade Coordinator

The 5th Grade Coordinator chairs the 5th Grade Graduation Committee and oversees the planning and execution of 5th grade senior activities. The 5th Grade Coordinator need not have a child currently enrolled in 5th grade. The duties of the 5th Grade Coordinator shall include but are not limited to the following:

a. Solicit input from upcoming 5th grade families to inform the committee's planning;

b. Recruit members for the 5th Grade Graduation Committee from the 5th grade parent community;

c. Submit an annual budget request through the Association's budget process and manage the 5th grade activity budget in coordination with the Treasurer;

d. Coordinate graduation-related fundraising;

e. Meet with the school administration in July and again in October of each school year to coordinate graduation-related activities;

f. Meet monthly with the President and the Fundraising team while school is in session to discuss upcoming activities and ensure alignment with broader Association operations.

*Operational details for the 5th Grade Graduation Committee, including the annual survey of 4th grade families, committee composition requirements, and funding mechanisms, are set forth in Proposal 10.*

### Member-at-Large

Two (2) Member-at-Large positions are reserved for parents or guardians whose child first enrolled at PS 193Q after the date of the most recent annual election. Members-at-Large are elected in October and serve on the executive board with voting rights. The duties of the Member-at-Large positions shall include but are not limited to:

a. Assist the Fundraising officers with planning and execution of fundraising activities;

b. Assist the Corresponding Secretary with the preparation and distribution of flyers and other communications;

c. Assist the Educational & Social Programming officer with the planning and execution of educational and social events;

d. Assist the Parent Volunteer Coordinator with volunteer recruitment and coordination.

### SLT (parent)

*Removed from Article IV.C and Article V.A in the finalized bylaws. The SLT parent member is not an officer of the Association. The SLT election process is retained in Article IV.E.*
